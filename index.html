Project overview

A responsive web app you can open from a URL, with playful flows such as custom routes, saved itineraries, and map-based check-ins—designed for clarity on phones first.

🎯

Who it’s for

New students and external visitors who want a fast mental map of buildings, services, and highlights.

🧭

What it does

Route ideas, place details, light tasks, and a more engaging way to explore than a plain map.

✨

Design focus

Mobile-first, playful, location-based, simple onboarding, accessible UI, campus storytelling.

Pick buildings, build a draft, then start check-in for an auto-ordered stop list. The map stays on the left; drafts and saved routes are on the right.

Pick stops Order Save / load

The three cards above are previews only. Tap Custom check-in route to plan and check in.

Route draft

Tap a building on the map, then choose Add to route in the panel. Keep at least one stop, pick a start point, and tap Start check-in for an ordered list. Walking paths follow real signage; here we only suggest floor-level hints.

My routes

Save your draft; use Load to restore stops, then Start check-in. Data stays in this browser.

Core features

Draft routes, save and reload them, and get a playful nearest-neighbour stop order on the illustrated map—extend with audio, AR, or badges later if you like.

🗺️

Smart route ideas

Use custom check-in to add buildings on the map and get an approximate visit order; the three themed cards set the mood.

📍

Check-in & digital stamps

Check in at highlights and collect stamps toward a simple digital “passport” you can share.

🎧

Bilingual audio (ready to plug in)

Room for EN/ZH narration so visitors understand what they’re looking at, not just where to walk.

🕹️

Micro-tasks & stories

Light prompts at each stop—find a desk, answer a trivia question, take a framed photo—to make the tour stick.

Experience highlights

Mobile-first layout, clear hierarchy, and task-led exploration so the tour feels playful and human-centric.
